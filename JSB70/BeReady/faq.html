Frequently Asked Questions

What is influenza (the flu)? Influenza is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses that infect the nose, throat, and sometimes the lungs.
How is the flu different from the common cold? The flu typically comes on suddenly and is more severe than the common cold, with symptoms like high fever, body aches, and fatigue.
Who is at higher risk for flu complications? High-risk groups include older adults, young children, pregnant women, and people with chronic medical conditions such as asthma, diabetes, or heart disease.
How effective is the flu vaccine? The flu vaccine is the best way to prevent flu and its complications. Its effectiveness varies each year, but it generally reduces the risk of flu illness by 40-60%.
Can the flu vaccine give you the flu? No, the flu vaccine cannot cause the flu. It contains inactivated (killed) viruses or a single gene from a flu virus (not the whole virus) to stimulate an immune response.
When should you get the flu vaccine? It's best to get the flu vaccine before flu season starts, typically from September to November, but getting vaccinated later can still be beneficial.
What are common side effects of the flu vaccine? Common side effects include soreness, redness, or swelling at the injection site, low-grade fever, and mild aches. These side effects are usually short-lived and less severe than flu symptoms.
